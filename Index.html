Introduction

First, learn VTG’s four modes of spinning (SS, TS, SO, TO). Understand the difference between wall, wheel, and floor plane.

Learn how to perform an isolation and a 4-petal antispin in wall & wheel plane without finger-spinning by passing through negative space and/or turning your body.

(I recommend teachers like DrexFactor and MCP, who have excellent tutorials on YouTube.)

Have you done all that? Great! Now you’re ready to learn the Double Staff Alphabet.

Each pictograph depicts a motion relationship between the three positions - Alpha, Beta, and Gamma. The DSA is laid out in this order to aid in memorization.

Pictographs are assigned letters for disambiguation. All positions and pictographs can be rotated or reflected any number of times while retaining the same letter designation. Right/Left hand can also be swapped.

The pattern in all letters A-V is as follows:

  • Two isolations
  • Two antispins
  • A hybrid of isolations and antispins

    You can use this pattern to navigate the DSA once you know a few letters.

    Any letter ending in Alpha (A-F) can be followed by any letter beginning in Alpha (A, B, C, J, K, L).

    Any letter ending in Beta (G-L) can be followed by any letter beginning in Beta (G, H, I, D, E, F)

    Any letter ending in Gamma (M-V) can be followed by any letter beginning in Gamma. (M-V)

    Here are some examples of words to spell:

    AABB
    GHI*4
    ALID
    NQNQ
    CAKE
    MOON
    EJEJ
    LGFA
    DKDK
    RRRS
    SPSP
    EKJD

    Double staff words are sometimes written inside parentheses (AABB). Positions are written with a lowercase p before the parentheses, such as with p(α), p(β), and p(Γ)

    Draw your sequences in the back of this book, and use red/blue pens both as staves and writing tools.

    Right hand = Red, Left Hand = Blue

    Have fun and good luck!
